---

# 📖 Introduction

Different applications have different networking requirements.

For example:

- Video Calls need low latency.
- Email tolerates delays.
- Streaming requires consistent bandwidth.

QoS ensures important traffic receives priority.

---

# 🚦 What is QoS?

Quality of Service (QoS) is a networking technique that prioritizes network traffic to improve performance.

---

# QoS Objectives

- Reduce Latency
- Minimize Packet Loss
- Improve Reliability
- Prioritize Critical Applications

---

# QoS Metrics

- Bandwidth
- Latency
- Jitter
- Packet Loss

---

# Traffic Prioritization

High Priority:

- Voice Calls
- Video Conferencing

Medium Priority:

- Business Applications

Low Priority:

- File Downloads
- Software Updates

---

# Congestion Management

Methods:

- Queue Management
- Traffic Shaping
- Traffic Policing
- Packet Scheduling

---

# QoS in Cloud Computing

Cloud providers optimize traffic using:

- Load Balancers
- Traffic Managers
- CDN
- Auto Scaling

---

# Real-World Example

During an online meeting, QoS prioritizes video and voice traffic over large file downloads, ensuring smooth communication.

---

# 💡 Best Practices

- Prioritize business-critical traffic.
- Monitor bandwidth usage.
- Minimize packet loss.
- Regularly review QoS policies.

---

# 📌 Key Takeaways

- QoS prioritizes network traffic.
- Latency and jitter affect voice/video quality.
- Traffic shaping controls bandwidth usage.
- QoS improves application performance.

---

# ❓ Interview Questions

### 1. What is QoS?

A technique for prioritizing network traffic.

### 2. Name four QoS metrics.

- Bandwidth
- Latency
- Jitter
- Packet Loss

### 3. What is jitter?

Variation in packet arrival time.

### 4. Why is QoS important?

To ensure critical applications receive sufficient network resources.

### 5. Which applications benefit most from QoS?

Voice and video conferencing.
